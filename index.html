Secrets of a Solo Traveller

Come on in from the cold search engines, take a seat. I'm Denise, recovering SEO specialist and marketer on a software engineering career change path.

I'm an avid solo traveller and love nothing more to take off alone with my little backpack. So in lieu of small talk, allow me to share some tips from my travels.

Below is a small selection of late-night hidden gems in some of my favourite cities. I highly recommend craning your neck around their doors if you're in the neighbourhood.

Shh! Don't tell anyone - let's keep them hidden : )

Don't say Beetlejuice in Minsk

Wonderful things happen when you crash local parties instead of following the guide books. Happily, I ended up in Beetlejuice, one of the capital city's quirkiest spots.

Drink with Locals in Lyon's Rebel Heart

A quick trip out to see a singer turned into gatecrashing a birthday before partying in an old warehouse apartment. Only in the Croix-Rousse, Lyon's revolutionary neighbourhood.

Get Ruined in Red in Budapest

Budapest's ruin bars have been thoroughly discovered. However, there are still some authentic spots where the beer is cheap, locals are moody and the music is excellent.

Swing out at Berlin's Best Hidden Bar

Saying Berlin is cool is grandma-suck-eggs. But this bar is next-level, or more precisely, Multi-layer cool. If you can find it, you'll have to take the term 'hanging out' very literally.

Belgrade's Boat Parties Start at Midnight

Someone burst into the hostel behind me and shouted "boat party!" We're leaving in ten." Ludicrous. I'd just arrived. It was past midnight. I hadn't unpacked. Did I go? Fuck yeah!

Make yourself at Haus in Hamburg's Schanze

Say nein to Reeperbahn. When it comes to partying with an edge, neighbouring district Sternschanze can't be missed. Haus 75 is the perfect bookend to your night.
